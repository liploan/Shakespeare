The Shakespeare Authorship Question

An Interactive Biographical, Historical, and Cryptographic Investigation

This scholarly forum is dedicated to exploring the historical, biographical, and cryptographic evidence surrounding the Shakespeare authorship controversy. By examining contemporary records, structural ciphers, and the cross-connections of Elizabethan literary circles, we seek to shed light upon the mystery of the golden age of English drama and the true identity of its primary genius.

The 1609 Sonnets Dedication Grid

Decrypting the 6-2-4 Structural Code

The dedication page of the 1609 edition of the Sonnets is divided into three inverted text blocks containing 6, 2, and 4 lines respectively, matching the letter counts of E-D-W-A-R-D D-E V-E-R-E.

Extracted Message:
"THESE SONNETS ALL BY EVER THE FORTH T"

In this key, "EVER" acts as a direct anagram of "E. VERE" (Edward de Vere), and "THE FORTH T" refers to the "Fourth T" added to the Triple Tau cross (de Vere's signature).

Stratford Monument Tampering

Comparing the 1656 Engraving vs. Modern Bust

Alexander Waugh and other researchers highlighted William Dugdale’s 1656 sketch of the Holy Trinity Church monument, which shows the Stratford man holding a sack of grain, contrasted with the modern writer-monument installed in the 1720s.
